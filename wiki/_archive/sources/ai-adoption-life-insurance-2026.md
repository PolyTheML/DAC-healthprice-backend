# AI Adoption in Life Insurance Underwriting (2026)

**Source**: [Pacific Life 2026 Underwriting Outlook Survey](https://www.lifehealth.com/2026-underwriting-outlook-ai-gains-ground-while-talent-gap-looms/) & Related Industry Analysis

**Type**: Market Research / Survey Data

**Ingested**: 2026-04-09

---

## Overview

Market-wide analysis of AI adoption in life insurance underwriting as of 2026. Shows rapid acceleration of AI deployment balanced against workforce skill gaps and talent availability concerns.

## AI Adoption Status

### Current Usage
- **45%** of insurers have AI as part of day-to-day workflows
  - **20%** use AI directly in routine work
  - **24%** use AI as decision-support tool
- **38%** still in pilot phase
- **17%** have not yet started

## Key Benefits (Ranked by Adoption)

| Benefit | % of Respondents |
|---------|------------------|
| Quicker underwriting decisions | 40% |
| Better use of medical & third-party data | 35% |
| Reduced manual review time | Implied |
| Improved consistency | Implied |

### Speed Gains
Underwriting reviews that once took **weeks** may now take **hours**.

## Data Source Priorities (Next 3-5 Years)

| Data Source | Impact | % |
|-------------|--------|-----|
| Electronic Health Records (EHRs) | Greatest | 52% |
| Prescription data | Moderate | 21% |
| Wearable health technology | Growing | 16% |

**Key Insight**: Medical records (EHRs) expected to have the greatest underwriting impact—directly relevant to your [Document Extraction & Medical Parsing](../topics/document-extraction.md) thesis.

## Workforce Challenges

### Talent Concerns
- **~70%** of respondents concerned about long-term underwriting talent availability

### Root Causes
| Issue | % |
|-------|-----|
| Aging workforce & loss of institutional knowledge | 38% |
| Balance automation with human expertise | 20% |
| Difficulty attracting younger professionals | 18% |

### Implication
This creates a "push" for agentic AI solutions: automation fills the talent gap while preserving human oversight.

## Industry Perspective (Pacific Life)

> "Underwriting is increasingly seen as an optimal first stop for a firm to begin integrating AI. It brings immense opportunities but also a caution: **AI is accelerating the process, not redefining the profession.**"

— Susan Ghaili, SVP and Chief Underwriter, Pacific Life

**Key Nuance**: AI augments underwriters, doesn't replace them.
