## Overview

An analysis of agentic AI's role in insurance, describing how autonomous AI agents collaborate with human underwriters and claims processors. Represents the next evolution beyond generative AI toward autonomous decision-making.

## What is Agentic AI?

**Definition**: AI systems that can act autonomously, make decisions, and execute tasks based on data analysis.

**Positioning**: Next evolution beyond generative AI (like [Claude](../entities/claude.md)), enabling more independent decision-making capabilities.

## How It Works with Human Underwriters

**Collaboration Model** (not replacement):
- Autonomous AI agents handle routine tasks and provide data-driven insights
- Employees focus on complex decision-making, exception handling, and strategic initiatives
- In underwriting specifically: "Frees up even more time for underwriters to focus on complex cases and final approvals"

### Workflow
1. AI analyzes vast amounts of data
2. AI creates risk profiles and personalized pricing recommendations
3. Human underwriter reviews complex or edge cases
4. Human makes final approval/decision

This is the [Human-in-the-Loop](../topics/human-in-the-loop.md) model: AI increases efficiency while humans retain judgment on high-stakes decisions.

## Straight-Through Processing (STP)

The article notes that agentic AI "increases straight-through processing by improving operational efficiency" in claims and underwriting contexts.

**Key Concept**: Routine, low-risk cases can be processed automatically end-to-end, freeing humans to focus on exceptions and complex cases.

## Current Industry Adoption
